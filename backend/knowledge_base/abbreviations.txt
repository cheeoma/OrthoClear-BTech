COMMON ABBREVIATIONS IN NIGERIAN ORTHOPAEDIC PHYSIOTHERAPY CLINICAL NOTES

PATIENT HISTORY ABBREVIATIONS:
c/o - complains of (the patient is saying they have this problem)
h/o - history of (the patient has had this problem before)
k/o - known case of (the patient is already diagnosed with this condition)
p/o - presenting complaint of (the main reason the patient came to the hospital)
HPC - History of Presenting Complaint (detailed story of the current problem)
PMH - Past Medical History (health problems the patient has had before)
FH - Family History (health problems that run in the patient's family)
SH - Social History (information about the patient's lifestyle, job and home)
ROS - Review of Systems (checking all parts of the body for problems)
LOC - Loss of Consciousness (the patient fainted or blacked out)
DOI - Date of Injury (the date when the injury happened)
MOI - Mechanism of Injury (how exactly the injury happened)

EXAMINATION ABBREVIATIONS:
ROM - Range of Motion (how far a joint can move in different directions)
AROM - Active Range of Motion (how far the patient can move the joint themselves)
PROM - Passive Range of Motion (how far the physiotherapist can move the joint for the patient)
MMT - Manual Muscle Test (a test to check how strong the muscles are)
SLR - Straight Leg Raise (a test where the leg is lifted straight to check for nerve problems)
SLRT - Straight Leg Raise Test (same as SLR, checks for nerve irritation in the lower back)
TTP - Tender to Palpation (it is painful when the physiotherapist presses on that area)
WNL - Within Normal Limits (the result is normal, nothing to worry about)
NAD - No Abnormality Detected (everything looks and feels normal)
OA - On Arrival (the condition of the patient when they first came in)
O/E - On Examination (what the physiotherapist found when they examined the patient)
Hx - History (the patient's medical background and story)
Dx - Diagnosis (the name of the condition the patient has been given)
Rx - Treatment or Prescription (the treatment plan given to the patient)
Fx - Fracture (a broken bone)
Sx - Symptoms (the problems and feelings the patient is experiencing)
Tx - Therapy or Treatment (the physiotherapy treatment being given)

DIAGNOSIS AND CONDITION ABBREVIATIONS:
OA - Osteoarthritis (wearing down of joint cartilage causing pain and stiffness)
RA - Rheumatoid Arthritis (an immune system disease that attacks and inflames joints)
LBP - Low Back Pain (pain in the lower part of the back)
CLBP - Chronic Low Back Pain (lower back pain that has lasted more than 3 months)
ALBP - Acute Low Back Pain (lower back pain that started recently, less than 6 weeks)
NP - Neck Pain (pain in the neck area)
SCI - Spinal Cord Injury (damage to the spinal cord causing loss of movement or feeling)
TBI - Traumatic Brain Injury (brain damage caused by a head injury)
CVA - Cerebrovascular Accident (a stroke, where blood flow to the brain is interrupted)
PID - Prolapsed Intervertebral Disc (a slipped disc in the spine pressing on nerves)
HNP - Herniated Nucleus Pulposus (another name for a slipped disc)
DDD - Degenerative Disc Disease (wearing down of the cushions between spinal bones)
ACL - Anterior Cruciate Ligament (a major stabilizing ligament inside the knee)
PCL - Posterior Cruciate Ligament (another stabilizing ligament at the back of the knee)
MCL - Medial Collateral Ligament (ligament on the inner side of the knee)
LCL - Lateral Collateral Ligament (ligament on the outer side of the knee)
PFPS - Patellofemoral Pain Syndrome (pain around the kneecap, common in runners)
ITB - Iliotibial Band (a thick band of tissue running down the outer thigh to the knee)
ITBS - Iliotibial Band Syndrome (irritation of the ITB causing outer knee pain)
RTC - Rotator Cuff (the group of four muscles that stabilize the shoulder joint)
RCST - Rotator Cuff Strain or Tear (injury to the shoulder stabilizing muscles)
SC - Spondylolisthesis (a condition where one spinal bone slips forward over another)
AS - Ankylosing Spondylitis (a type of arthritis that mainly affects the spine)
CRPS - Complex Regional Pain Syndrome (severe long lasting pain usually in an arm or leg)
DVT - Deep Vein Thrombosis (a dangerous blood clot forming in a deep vein usually in the leg)
MS - Multiple Sclerosis (a disease affecting the brain and spinal cord causing weakness)
CP - Cerebral Palsy (a condition affecting movement and posture from birth or early childhood)

TREATMENT AND PROCEDURE ABBREVIATIONS:
PT - Physiotherapy or Physical Therapy (treatment using exercises and physical techniques)
OT - Occupational Therapy (treatment to help patients do daily activities independently)
TENS - Transcutaneous Electrical Nerve Stimulation (a machine that uses mild electric current to reduce pain)
IFT - Interferential Therapy (a deeper electrical treatment to reduce pain and swelling)
SWD - Short Wave Diathermy (a heat treatment using radio waves to treat deep tissue pain)
MWD - Microwave Diathermy (heat treatment using microwaves for muscle and joint pain)
US - Ultrasound Therapy (sound wave treatment used to heal soft tissue injuries)
IR - Infrared Radiation (heat lamp treatment used to relax muscles and reduce pain)
HEP - Home Exercise Program (exercises the patient is told to do at home)
MFR - Myofascial Release (a hands-on technique to release tightness in muscles and connective tissue)
MHP - Moist Heat Pack (a warm wet pack applied to reduce muscle stiffness and pain)
CP - Cold Pack (an ice pack applied to reduce swelling and pain after injury)
NMES - Neuromuscular Electrical Stimulation (electrical treatment to help weak muscles work again)
PNF - Proprioceptive Neuromuscular Facilitation (an advanced stretching technique to improve flexibility)
STM - Soft Tissue Mobilization (hands-on massage and movement to reduce muscle tightness)
JM - Joint Mobilization (gentle movement of a joint by the physiotherapist to improve its range)
MT - Manual Therapy (hands-on treatment techniques used by physiotherapists)
Ex - Exercises (physical exercises prescribed as part of treatment)
NWB - Non Weight Bearing (the patient must not put any weight on that limb)
PWB - Partial Weight Bearing (the patient can put some but not full weight on that limb)
FWB - Full Weight Bearing (the patient can put their full body weight on that limb)
WB - Weight Bearing (putting body weight through a limb)
WBAT - Weight Bearing As Tolerated (put as much weight as is comfortable)

MEASUREMENT AND GRADING ABBREVIATIONS:
VAS - Visual Analogue Scale (a pain scale from 0 to 10 where 0 is no pain and 10 is worst pain)
NRS - Numeric Rating Scale (similar to VAS, patient rates pain from 0 to 10)
BMI - Body Mass Index (a number calculated from height and weight to check if weight is healthy)
BP - Blood Pressure (the pressure of blood flowing through your arteries)
HR - Heart Rate (how many times your heart beats per minute)
RR - Respiratory Rate (how many times you breathe per minute)
MRC - Medical Research Council Scale (a scale used to grade muscle strength from 0 to 5)
ADL - Activities of Daily Living (everyday tasks like dressing, bathing, cooking and walking)
IADL - Instrumental Activities of Daily Living (more complex daily tasks like cooking and managing money)

BODY POSITION AND DIRECTION ABBREVIATIONS:
R - Right side of the body
L - Left side of the body
B/L - Bilateral (both sides of the body)
U/L - Unilateral (one side of the body only)
Ant - Anterior (front of the body)
Post - Posterior (back of the body)
Med - Medial (towards the middle of the body)
Lat - Lateral (towards the outside of the body)
Sup - Superior (upper part or above)
Inf - Inferior (lower part or below)
Prox - Proximal (closer to the center of the body)
Dist - Distal (further away from the center of the body)
